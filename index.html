how to use you new kit

A simple step‑by‑step guide with pictures, a checklist, and a quick quiz. Perfect for beginners!

What is coco coir?
It’s the soft, brown “fluff” made from coconut husks. It helps hold water and gives roots air.

🧰 What you need

Tools

  • Small pots (with holes)
  • Tray or plate (to catch drips)
  • Spray bottle or small watering can
  • Labels (or lolly sticks)

Materials

  • Coco coir block or loose coco coir
  • Seeds
  • Warm water
  • Optional: a little compost / plant food later
Picture idea:

Draw a bucket, warm water, and a coco coir block turning into fluffy soil.

✅ Step-by-step (tap to open)

1 Make coco coir fluffy ➕
  • Put the coco coir block in a small bucket, pot or bowl.
  • Add warm water (not boiling). Wait 10–20 minutes.
  • Use clean hands or a fork to fluff it up.
  • It should feel like a wrung‑out sponge: damp, not dripping.
Beginner tip:

If it’s too wet, squeeze a handful gently to remove extra water.

2 Fill your pots ➕
  • Fill each pot with damp coco coir.
  • Tap the pot lightly (don’t squash it hard).
  • Leave a small gap at the top (about 1 cm) for watering.
3 Plant the seeds ➕
  • Make a tiny hole with your finger or pencil.
  • Put the seed in the hole.
  • Cover it lightly with coco coir.
  • Spray with water so the seed stays in place.
Quick rule for depth:
Many seeds like to be planted about 2× as deep as the seed is wide. Always check your seed packet if you have one.
Picture idea:

Draw a pot, a seed under the soil, then a tiny sprout popping up.

4 Label + place somewhere nice ➕
  • Write the plant name and date on a label.
  • Put pots somewhere warm and bright (windowsill is great).
  • Some seeds like a clear lid or cling film to keep moisture in (poke tiny air holes).
5 Watering & aftercare ➕
  • Keep coco coir damp, not soggy.
  • Use a spray bottle for tiny seeds so they don’t wash away.
  • When leaves appear, give more light so seedlings don’t stretch.
  • Coco coir has very few nutrients, so later you can add weak plant feed (once seedlings have a few leaves).
Safety note: Wash hands after gardening. Ask an adult before using fertiliser/plant feed.

🧪 Mini science bit (for school projects)

Coco coir helps because it holds water like a sponge and also leaves little air gaps for roots. Seeds need water, warmth, and air to start growing.

Try this: Plant the same seeds in two pots: one with coco coir and one with compost. Keep watering the same. Write what you see each day (sprout date, height, leaf count).
